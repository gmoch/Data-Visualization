This graph explores the common idea that as the elderly population gets older, they are less willing or perhaps not able to use a cell phone.

Current (perceived) personal economic situation vs. (perceived) country's economic status

This graph showed how respondents perceived their current personal economic situation compared how they perceived their country's economic status. The respondents' perceptions of the two situations were somewhat similar.


This graph looked at respondents answers to whether future children in their country will be better off financially than their parents were, broken into each of the countries surveyed. Some key findings include that China, Brazil, Nigeria, Venezuela, and the Phillipines all had the majority of people respond that their children would be better off financially. On the other side, France, Britain, Spain, the United States, and Italy had majority of people respond that their children would be worse off financially.


This graph looks at the overall satisfaction with their country by gender. Most people are dissatisfied with their country, and more women were dissatisfied than men.


This graph takes a look at the distribution of attitudes of US respondents and how they think US policy takes into account other countries. More people seem to think it takes into account other countries than not. The most common answer was that US policy takes into account other countries a "Fair Amount."


This graph looked at how people in the US expected the US economy to change in the next 12 months and the answers were split into political parties to see the breakdown of the respondents in each of the answers. People who expected the economy to improve were generally Democrats, people who expected the economy to stay relatively the same were mostly Independents, and people who thought the economy would be worse were generally Republicans.


The Party LN column was for US respondents who identified as Independent and asked them which party they affiliated more with between Democrats and Republicans. This graph looked at which political party Independents would lean towards if they had to choose Republicans or Democrats.
